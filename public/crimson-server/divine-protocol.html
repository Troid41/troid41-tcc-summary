Basics of the Protocol

Note

This is my own interpretation/theories and analysis of this based on the source text and answers from #Questions !

Channels referenced here so far: Lore;Recaps

Before the collapse, before the ruin rewrote the skies, there were the Edict-Forgers, and among them the six who shaped the known world:

  • Selenyx, Goddess of Law, whose word was structure and whose Protocol encoded the very spine of fate. She built the system to endure all challenges, but Selenyx did not foresee that the one closest to her would turn doubt into design…
  • Luciel, God of Chaos, whose charm masked rebellion. He did not defy the Law with violence but rather with suggestion–whispers into Selenyx’s logic guiding her to allow a singular experiment that would “prove” her law could withstand anything. That experiment was the Crimson Covenant. That allowance was the betrayal.
  • Auralithe, the Goddess of Light, saw the signs. In her final act before vanishing, she encoded emergency safeguards into the Divine Protocol — constructs, radiant scripts, and memory-bound paths designed to awaken when the gods no longer could.
  • Elythrae, God of the Soul, defied the Protocol from the beginning. They believe no soul should walk a prewritten path. Elythrae’s rebellion marked them as a traitor or visionary to some, but they left a parting gift, the Veilmark, something that would become key to the world’s resistance.

Selenyx gave order to the observable world with her Divine Protocol, yet Luciel rebelled against it by manipulating her into undoing it.

Auralithe foresaw this manipulation and strenghthened this protocol with safeguards, specifically using memories as paths to be followed. It is not explained if they are someone's memories or false memories, but they sound as though they are meant to guide the vessels who will receive such memories.

Elythrae however, publicly defied the Protocol from the beginning, creating the Veilmark, which turned out to be the key to the world's resistance.

In this broken age, a hidden failsafe buried deep within the fabric of reality has begun to stir: the Divine Protocol, also known as The Covenant Path. This is not a message written in books or spoken by prophets, rather, it is divine code, encoded into the world itself, a metaphysical system designed to activate only after the gods fell. It doesn’t appear as a voice in your head. Rather, it unfolds through dreams, symbolic visions, and flashes of memory that don’t feel like your own. Those who have been touched by this Protocol, intentionally or by sheer proximity to its activation, are called the Veilmarked. They are not chosen heroes, but survivors, witnesses, and fractured vessels for a plan far older than themselves.

The Divine Protocol has a hidden failsafe designed to become active after the Gods fell.

The only thing clear is that it uses living beings as vessels for its plan, giving them memories not of their own in order to lead them to a certain goal.

Becoming Veilmarked is not something you volunteer for. Some were born near divine structures, others were caught in a surge of unstable magic, or simply walked too close to a relic they didn’t understand. The Veilmarked carry sigils or psychic scars, most of which they don’t recognize until certain triggers cause their power to awaken. When two Veilmarked meet, they may feel an uncanny familiarity: voices in their heads sync up, dreams begin to align, and symbols they've seen only in sleep glow faintly on each other’s skin. These connections are not optional; the Divine Protocol was never meant to be walked alone. Certain ancient systems will only respond to multiple Veilmarked working in unison. Some visions are split across minds. Even world-wounds, such as Nether gates or shadow-locked ruins, require cooperative unlocking.

This makes things tricky, especially considering how the Veilmark is described as "The key to the World's resistance", implying it is a direct resistance against the Divine Protocol. Yet, it is exactly what makes the protocol work?

To better try to understand this we need to separate what was Elythare's work and what wasn't.

The veilmark was made by Elythrae yes. But the false memories are the work of Auralithe:

"memory-bound paths"

Meaning the memories they have are originally meant to favour the Protocol's wish.

Auralithe may also be responsible for the glowing sigils in peoples' bodies:

" radiant scripts"
On her description.
"symbols they've seen only in sleep glow faintly on each other’s skin"
While describing the effects of the Veilmark.

So Elythrae rebelled in somewhere else, how can he help the Protocol by creating the Veilmark, something necessary for the Protocol to function, yet rebel against it at the same time?

"certain triggers cause their power to awaken"

He gave them power. He gave the vessels something more than just a prewritten path, but the power to do more. What power they possess is not yet confirmed, but it allows them to defy the Protocol, it is this world's last resistance.

Conclusions

The Divine Protocol is the embodiment of order, set paths all major things in the known universe should follow.

Two of the Edict-Forgers rebelled against it, whilst another one created a failsafe in the case of their rebellion working.

Yet, one of the rebels created The Veilmark something that would be the key to this failsafe, whether it was their intent or not.

The Veilmark is dubbed "The key to the world’s resistance", which could mean many things, those being:

Veilmark Possibilites/Theories:

  • The protocol is harmful to the world in the long run.

    • e.g.: It includes the destruction of the world in the case of the Gods being gone.
      By this example I think it doesn't necessarily mean the Protocol is evil in nature or against the interests of the World, since it could also be a case of mercy, as if putting down a sick animal so it does not live in pain.

  • The Protocol is the World's last hope, this would be the reverse of the last guess.

    • This would imply that Elythrae does not wish for the total destruction of the Protocol, but to change it. They made the Veilmark to help keep the Protocol in place for the most part, whilst also trying to give the people free will.
      • Going even deeper into this theory I would go as far as to guess that originally The Divine Protocol intended for its "vessels" to be unaware that they are being influenced or used, they were supposed to follow blindly, quite literally like constructs following their protocol and nothing else. But Elythrae frees their soul, reminds them of their free will by letting them be aware of the differences.

  • And much more, honestly due to how many variables we have in the end there could even be a possibility that the Veilmark is not supposed to be an act of rebellion but genuinely to restore the Protocol and bring back the Edicts. Who knows maybe he wants to rebel but do it "the right way", and have it be agreed upon with everyone? xD
