Server Launch Recap – Day One

(A voice echoes across the realms — fractured, ancient, and reverent. It is neither god nor mortal, but something caught in between…)

“Hear this, Veilmarked. The silence is broken. The Protocol awakens.

Long have the realms drifted, severed from the Edict-Forgers' breath, rotting beneath the weight of forgotten law. But within you stirs the flicker — the Mark — that which even death could not bind.

You stand now at the edge of all things. The world remembers your name not, but the Covenant does.

Rise, Echo-Bearers. The Witherwood whispers. The Divine Forgeries rumble. And the Path winds forward through ash and revelation.

This is no blessing. This is the burden of remembrance.

Welcome to The Crimson Covenant. The Sundering broke the world. You… must decide what becomes of it.”

With this haunting invocation, the realms stirred, and the Veilmarked awoke.

The Descent

Players found themselves within a mysterious holding chamber — cold, echoing, and humming with divine residue. Without warning, the ground trembled beneath their feet.

“The ground trembles… a deep rumble rises from below, as if the world might collapse.”

The floor gave way, plunging them into the open sea. In the dark waters, violet light shimmered ahead. A message appeared:

“Swim now. Follow the glowing Chosen — they will guide the way. Seek the village that cradles the Witherwood tree.”

The Shore & Gathering

Drawn to safety by a strange glow, the players emerged on a shoreline beneath a gray sky, where a group of Veilmarked — perhaps chosen earlier, perhaps simply more aware — took the lead. They gathered, rallied, and ventured through the dense forest toward the village where the Witherwood awaited.

Upon arrival, the newcomers found a small camp nestled at the village’s edge. Fires were lit. Stories shared. Names exchanged. Tents claimed. It was here, among whispers of something vast and ancient, that the Veilmarked began to piece together fragments of their purpose.

First Tools, First Questions

Though answers were scarce, the locals offered guidance — particularly Jorin and Coleen, two figures with an unsettling awareness of the Protocol’s movements. They provided basic tools and ensured the newcomers could defend themselves and gather what they needed to survive.

Yet beneath every tool handed over and every answer given, more questions stirred:

  • Why did the Divine Protocol choose them?
  • What is the Mark’s purpose?
  • Who are the Chosen… and who were left behind?
  • Is this fate… or something far more sinister?

The Covenant has only just begun to unfold. The realms remain fractured, the gods absent, and the truth buried deep beneath the soil and soul alike.

What Comes Next

The coming days promise deeper truths — and greater trials. The Veilmarked have taken their first steps, but the Path is long, and the world waits, silent and watching.

"Rise, Echo-Bearers... the burden of remembrance has found you."

Event Recap – Ashes of the Rootbound

Phase Thread: Divine Protocol Deviation Detected

It began with whispers...
A diseased cow found at the edge of the Witherwood - skinned.

The villagers chose silence. But the Veilmarked remembered.
And the Divine Protocol stirred, whispering:

"Observation error. Memory thread reactivation initiated."

Urged by instinct, the Veilmarked were pulled back to the old campfire, where their journey began. There, reflection turned to tension. Smoke drifted unseen. Something was out there. Watching. Waiting.

"Proceed with caution. The timeline may already be tainted."

Smoke after the flame

The air was still, but uneasy.
The Protocol whispered, faint and uncertain, as Veilmarked returned to the campfire - the place where it all began.

“The Divine Protocol stirs… a buried flame calls to you.”
“Your thoughts drift to the old campfire…”

And with it came back the memories.

“You remember the cow they found in the woods. Not dead. Not alive. Just… skinned.”
“Its body stripped clean, as if peeled by something that had done it before.”

There were too many footsteps that day. Too close. And yet... no one went further.

“You told yourself it wasn't your concern.”
“You were wrong.”

The Veilmarked began to question. Tensions rose. The Protocol sharpened.

“Observation Error: Site left unchecked”
“Memory Thread Reactivation: Skinned entity | Traces of presence”
→ RECOMMENDED ACTION: Return to the site. Examine the trail. Follow the silence.

Then... they smelled it. Smoke - but no fire. No warmth. Just something waiting in the clearing.

“Trace particles detected: Char | Ash | Residual Heat”

And then, as the players rallied -

Whispering Smoke

“The Divine Protocol reinitializes.”
“Phase Sequence: STAGE ONE – Journey to the Woods”
"Smell recognized: Smoke – human-made origin likely"
→ NEW DIRECTIVE: Follow the source of the smoke.

The Veilmarked set out into the forest.
There, within the blackened glade, they uncovered a burnt child’s hat?

“We were just cold… he said we’d feel warm soon…”

Their voices lingered, even as the players gathered their remains.
The whispers gave way to a form.

Ashen Child

“He said the fire would take the sickness… but the sickness stayed… now it’s in me.”

A partially-burnt child emerged from the smoke, animated by something far from peace.

The Protocol gave its grim order:

“Unburden the child… Grant it peace.”

Defeated, the child fell, but not alone. From the soot and grief came a wail that split the trees.

Ashen Mother

“No… no… my baby…”
“You let the fire take him, Tomas… and now it burns me.”

The Ashen Mother appeared, twisted by loss, consumed by rage.
Her voice cracked the world.

“I will not rest. Not until the world feels what I felt.”

“Protocol Directive: Purge the burning sorrow. Let grief turn to ash.”

The players struck her down, but what lingered behind was not closure. It was dread. The Mother fell, not with peace - but fury.
The woods were silent again… but far from safe.

Breaking into Tomas’ Butcher Shop

Returned to the village, the players turned to Tomas’ shop, now locked tight and suspiciously abandoned.

“The door is barred… it hasn’t been opened in days.”

Inside: crates hastily packed. One crate, left ajar, begged investigation. Within it? A journal containing the truth he tried to bury...

System Shift – Divine Interference Confirmed

“The flame of truth has been kindled. Shadows linger beneath the surface.”
“Easy Mode unlocked: Hunger enabled. Hostile mobs now spawn in the wilds. Stay vigilant.”

The Protocol, once steady, now hesitates.
Luciel’s voice - chaos incarnate - has slipped through.

The timeline is no longer safe.

Though unease lingers, the Veilmarked return to routine.
But the question now looms: How long will this quiet last?

The Divine Protocol watches. Your path is far from over.

Event Recap - The Tower Awakens

Phase Thread: Memory Convergence Logged

It began in silence…
Not a whisper this time, but a breath held too long by the world itself.

The villagers spoke of hammering in the dark, sparks striking against nothing — and by morning, the earth had also seemingly grown a spine of stone:

A library where none had been... A tower that should not exist...

Elder Thorne's Warning

The air shifted. Elder Thorne’s voice — once brittle and scattered — sharpened like flint struck against stone.

“There’s been an odd convergence… northeast of the tavern.”
“Necromancers. Restless dead. Gathering where they shouldn’t.”

He didn’t shout. He didn’t need to.
The forest itself seemed to lean in.

“Something with power enough to call them stirs in the woods.”
“If you value peace — or your life — investigate.”

## A Mind Awakens
The wind stopped.

Something deeper than silence stretched through the forest — a pause too still to be natural.

A hush falls over the woods. Even the wind holds its breath.

Then, a blade of light carved down from above —
silver and searing, cutting clean through canopy and shadow alike.

The world held its breath as something spoke.
A voice, ancient and distant, echoes from everywhere and nowhere.

“Eirwen… awaken.”

Discovering the Hidden Tower

The forest shifted.

What once felt like underbrush became something older — watching.

The forest thickened. Branches clawed at you whilst shadows lengthen unnaturally.
Mist gathered at one's feet, swirling with whispers only some seem to hear.

Suddenly, the trees parted — revealing a tower of spiraling stone, jutting from the earth…
Its form echoes the shrine west of the village — but this is no shrine.

The Protocol thrummed. Recognition?
No — anticipation.

A bell tolled from nowhere.
Once. Clear. Too distant, yet near — like a memory resurfacing.

Something here remembers you.
Even if you do not remember it.

Meeting Elarion – The Voice Beyond the Doors

The towering structure looms before you, its doors sealed tight like the vault of a forgotten god.

A voice resonates from beyond the black veil — soft, distorted, and cold.

“You arrive as the Protocol foretold…” the voice intones. “I am Elarion…”
The words vibrate through the forest air, as if spoken directly into your mind.

Your thoughts tangle with whispers: fragmented words of past visitors, their fates uncertain.

“Enter,” the voice whispers again, softer now. “Let the Tower judge your worth.”

Beginning the Brews

Elarion’s eyes glint as he hands you a battered tome: the Brewing Journal.

“Three brews mark your initiation,” he intones, voice echoing oddly within the Tower’s walls. “Mundane. Thick. Awkward. Each tests a brewer’s heart — and each reveals a truth.”

He leans forward, robes shifting like liquid shadow.

“These are not mere potions. They are the first step in binding your will to the Tower’s memory.”

A low hum pulses through the floor beneath your feet, as if the Tower itself listens.

“Do not falter,” Elarion murmurs, voice like distant thunder. “Every drop you brew writes itself into the Tower’s memory.”

Completion of All Three Brews

As the last potion cools, the Tower’s walls resonate with a low, musical hum.

A ripple of light climbs the tower’s inner walls, each floor above flickering briefly to life.

A low chime rings in your mind as arcane symbols bloom and fade across the floor beneath you.

Elarion nods slowly, his smile curling at the edges. “You have done well… the Tower acknowledges you.”

Only the First Step

The Divine Protocol thrums low within your chest.

Recommendation: Remain vigilant. The Tower has many more floors — and many truths to uncover.

The Tower’s walls quiet, but the air still hums with a promise — and a warning.

Somewhere far above, something ancient stirs in response to your progress...
